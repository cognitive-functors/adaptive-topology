### Lemma 1: Necessity of Time (T)

**Statement:** Any thought is in time.

**Proof of Lemma 1:**

Thought M must either:

**(a) be a memory** — refer to past experience (T = Past)
**(b) be a perception** — occur in the present moment (T = Present)
**(c) be an anticipation** — refer to the future (T = Future)

**Why are other options impossible?**

- A thought outside the flow of time = a thought that **does not occur** at any moment
- But "to occur" is the definition of existence in time
- If M satisfies neither (a), nor (b), nor (c) → M **does not occur** → M **is not a thought**

**Contradiction.** Therefore, **T is ineliminable**. ∎

### Lemma 2: Necessity of Scale (D)

**Statement:** Any thought has a level of abstraction.

**Proof of Lemma 2:**

Thought M must either:

**(a) be concrete** — relate to a specific object/event (D = Concrete)
**(b) be abstract** — relate to a category/pattern (D = Abstract)
**(c) be meta-level** — relate to the very structure of thinking (D = Meta)

**Why are other options impossible?**

**Contradiction.** Therefore, **D is ineliminable**. ∎

** IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION: Embodied vs Cognitive Empathy**

There's a critical difference between **real access to agency** (embodied empathy) and **cognitive simulation** (cognitive empathy):

**Embodied Empathy (2nd position NLP, genuine):**
- Person **physically and cognitively enters** other's state (bodily sensations, emotions, perspective)

- Result: **I feel his pressure** (not thinking about him, but **experiencing**)
- → In C4 terms: temporary shift to state F⟨*,*,Other⟩ **with full access to agency**

**Cognitive "Empathy" (cognitive simulation, false):**
- Person **thinks** they understand other, but does so **from their position** (A=Self)
- This is **attribution** ("he probably feels X"), not **experience**
- Result: **I think he feels pressure** (intellectual hypothesis, not empathy)
- → In C4 terms: staying in F⟨*,*,Self⟩, merely **thinking about** F⟨*,*,Other⟩

**How to Check:**
1. Ask yourself: "**Do I feel his feeling?**" (embodied) or "**Do I think about his feeling?**" (cognitive)
2. Are there **bodily sensations** (tension, breathing, gestures) like the other person's?
3. Can I **answer on his behalf** with his intonation and logic? (embodied) or only **describe** his logic from outside? (cognitive)

**Connection to Agency:**
- **2nd position NLP (embodied)** = real access to agency A=Other (trains capacity for F⟨*,*,Other⟩)
- **Cognitive simulation** ≠ access to agency (staying in A=Self, merely **modeling** Other cognitively)

**Practical Conclusion:**
If person **cannot enter embodied state** of another (only cognitively simulates), their A-axis is limited → **low coverage in agency** → difficulties in empathy, negotiation, coaching.
